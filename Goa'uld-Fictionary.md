# The Goa'uld Fictionary: Swadesh Foundation & Semantic Extensions

**The Fictionary is the English-language companion to the official Goa'uld Dictionary.** Where the canonical vocabulary excels at military commands, ritual phrases, and titles, the fundamental human experience — body parts, numbers, nature, kinship, colors, emotions — is largely undocumented. The Fictionary fills these gaps systematically using the *Swadesh list* (a universal framework of core vocabulary shared by all human languages) and extends the official lexicon into modern contexts such as software, technology, and abstract reasoning.

Every entry derives from attested canonical roots. No new phonological pattern is introduced without precedent in the corpus. Compound logic follows canonical rules: the apostrophe marks morpheme boundaries, the suffix *-le* augments, *-ia* negates, *-p* pluralizes, and *-k* means "to be." Two new productive roots are introduced with full etymological justification — **sha** (inner feeling, from *Kel'sha*: "It will be so," implying inner acceptance) and **ren** (breath, from the Egyptian substrate root *rn*, consistent with the show's documented etymological sources).

## How the Fictionary works

Three construction strategies documented in the canonical corpus itself are employed throughout.

**Semantic compounding** joins canonical roots via the head-modifier pattern. *Tar* (human) + *-ma* (life/feminine suffix) → *Tar'ma* (woman). *Kor* (hard/iron) + *tal* (earth/part) → *Kor'tal* (stone). *Mel* (fire/ending) + *keka* (death, Unas) → *Mel'keka* (ash — the death of fire). Every apostrophe marks a morpheme boundary.

**Vowel shifting** marks semantic distance, following the attested *nok/nokia* pattern and the demonstrative pair *haka* (this, near) / *hako* (that, far). The *a → o* shift encodes proximity vs. distance, presence vs. absence.

**Unas inheritance** honors the canonical origin of Goa'uld on P3X-888. Unas roots *ska nat* (preserve life), *keka* (death), *no na* (home) survive and anchor many compounds.
